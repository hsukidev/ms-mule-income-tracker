# `Income` and `WorldIncome` stay split; **Format Preference** lifts to its own seam

`src/modules/income.tsx` and `src/modules/worldIncome.ts` look duplicative at a glance — both compute meso totals over the same `MuleBossSlate.from(selectedBosses, worldGroup)` call. A unified "roster aggregator" module that subsumed both has been considered. We are deliberately not building it: the two answer different questions at different aggregation grains. `Income.of` returns **Potential Income** (uncapped, per-**Mule** or summed roster) — the **Drawer** and **Character Card** headline. `WorldIncome.of` runs the **World Cap Cut** and attributes survivors back to **Mules** — the **KPI Card** bignum, **PieChart Card** slices, **Cap Drop Badge**. CONTEXT.md explicitly distinguishes **Potential Meso** (uncapped, stable for planning) from **Contributed Meso** (post-cut); a unified module would conflate them at the call site, which is the opposite of what the language asks for.

The real shallow seam in these files is **Format Preference** ferrying. Both `Income` (as a class field) and `useWorldIncome` (via `Object.assign`) plumb `{ abbreviated, toggle }` through `IncomeContext`, and `useAutoFullFormatOnZero` is a cross-module side-effect rule that lives in `income.tsx` but fires off **Total Weekly Income**. Lifting **Format Preference** into its own provider (state + the **Auto-Fullformat-On-Zero Rule**) cleans this up without touching the income split. The income classes become plain data; callers thread format preference at the render boundary.

This adds a fourth persisted context (alongside World, Theme, Density), which is the trigger ADR-0001 named for revisiting the persisted-context factory. We are still not building the factory: **Format Preference** carries its own asymmetry (the auto-zero auto-flip rule has no analog in the other three), so the factory's option surface still ends up wider than what it abstracts. ADR-0001's stance is preserved with one new parallel provider.

## Considered Options

- **Unify `Income` and `WorldIncome` into one roster-aggregator module.** Rejected: conflates **Potential Income** with **Contributed Meso** at call sites; the two queries serve different surfaces (Drawer/Card headline vs KPI/Pie/Badge); the deletion test on `WorldIncome` shows ~50 LOC of cap-cut algorithm scattering — it's already deep.
- **Lift **Format Preference** to its own provider; flatten `Income` to a number; keep `WorldIncome` as-is (chosen).** Income classes carry only aggregation; **Format Preference** state, the **Auto-Fullformat-On-Zero Rule**, and the toggle live in one place. Locality of the format-preference triple improves; the income split's semantics stay intact.
- **Build the persisted-context factory now that there's a fourth provider.** Deferred: **Format Preference** doesn't share the storage skeleton cleanly (auto-zero auto-flip is unique to it). Re-evaluate if a fifth persisted context lands.

If a future surface needs both **Potential Meso** and **Contributed Meso** at the same call site (e.g. a per-mule "you'd earn X uncapped, you contribute Y" comparison), revisit by introducing a thin presenter that composes the two — not by merging the modules.
